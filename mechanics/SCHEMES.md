# Schemes

**Status:** WIP

## Purpose

Schemes are intermediate strategic projects that define **how** an organization pursues a Plot.

They bridge the gap between long-term ambitions (Plots) and concrete actions (Missions).

---

## Hierarchy

Organization
→ Plot (**What do we want?**)
→ Scheme (**How do we pursue it?**)
→ Mission (**What do we do right now?**)

This creates a clean separation of responsibilities:

- **Plots create direction.**
- **Schemes create strategy.**
- **Missions consume resources.**

---

## Core Concept

Schemes are not simply stages or progress bars.

A Scheme represents a chosen strategic approach toward advancing a Plot.

Different Schemes pursuing the same Plot may create different opportunities, risks, and outcomes.

---

## Examples

### Plot
Control the High Spire.

Possible Schemes:

- Political Infiltration
- Criminal Expansion
- Economic Domination
- Occult Corruption

Each Scheme advances the same Plot through different methods.

---

## Strategic Choice

Schemes should create meaningful decisions.

Players choose:

- Which Scheme to pursue.
- Which approaches fit their organization.
- Whether to remain hidden or act aggressively.
- Which risks they are willing to accept.

Choosing one Scheme may prevent or delay others.

---

## Scheme Effects

Schemes may:

- Unlock mission pools.
- Generate new opportunities.
- Increase exposure.
- Create rival responses.
- Alter world state.
- Enable unique rewards or consequences.

---

## Mission Pools

Schemes generate or unlock missions aligned with their approach.

Example:

### Scheme: Political Infiltration

Possible missions:

- Bribe officials
- Gather blackmail
- Forge alliances
- Spread propaganda

### Scheme: Criminal Expansion

Possible missions:

- Extort businesses
- Eliminate rivals
- Smuggle weapons
- Intimidate witnesses

This allows content reuse while creating different playstyles.

---

## Capabilities

Schemes may prefer certain capabilities without requiring hard assignments.

Example:

Political Infiltration favors:

- Influence
- Wealth
- Intel

Criminal Expansion favors:

- Violence
- Logistics
- Fear

Missions executed under the Scheme naturally benefit from matching capabilities.

---

## Resource Commitment

Schemes generally do **not** directly consume Assets or Crews.

Resource commitment occurs primarily at the Mission level.

This preserves:

- Flexibility
- Clarity
- Existing assignment systems

Avoid:

Asset → Scheme → Mission

Prefer:

Asset → Mission

---

## Lieutenant Champions

Schemes may optionally be championed by a Lieutenant.

Championing a Scheme may:

- Provide bonuses.
- Unlock unique missions.
- Increase prestige on success.
- Damage reputation on failure.
- Generate internal politics.

Championing is optional, not mandatory.

---

## Internal Politics

Lieutenant champions create emergent stories:

- Rival lieutenants compete for influence.
- Successful champions accumulate power.
- Failures weaken loyalty.
- Organizations may favor or sideline ambitious subordinates.

This reinforces the mafia fantasy without adding new systems.

---

## Visibility & Exposure

Schemes may have visibility states:

- Hidden
- Suspected
- Exposed

Progressing a Scheme often increases exposure and invites interference.

Rivals may discover:

- The existence of a Scheme.
- Its approach.
- Its objectives.
- Weak points to exploit.

---

## Counterplay

Rival factions may:

- Interfere with missions.
- Launch counter-schemes.
- Increase exposure.
- Delay progress.
- Manipulate world conditions.

Counterplay should slow or complicate progress rather than erase it.

---

## Design Principles

- Schemes answer: **How do we achieve our goals?**
- Schemes create strategic identity.
- Schemes unlock missions rather than replace them.
- Resource commitment belongs primarily to Missions.
- Existing systems should be reused whenever possible.
- Different paths should create different stories.

---

## Avoid

- Pure progress bars.
- Mandatory lieutenant assignment.
- Permanent asset assignment.
- Additional resource systems.
- Mission checklists disguised as Schemes.

A Scheme should create choices, not chores.

---

## Open Questions

- How many active Schemes can an organization maintain?
- Can multiple Schemes support the same Plot simultaneously?
- Can Schemes change mid-execution?
- Can rival factions hijack or steal Schemes?
- Should some Schemes be faction-specific?
- How does AI choose Schemes?
