# Heat System (Faction Pressure Model)

**Status:** WIP

## Mechanic Summary

Heat represents the level of institutional attention and enforcement pressure directed at the criminal faction. It is not a simple “visibility” meter, but a systemic measure of how aggressively the world is attempting to suppress the organization.

The system operates across multiple scales: global (faction), local (territories), and personal (characters via Exposure). Together, these layers create a structured escalation model that shapes how safely the player can expand, operate, and use their assets.

## Purpose

This mechanic exists to:

- Prevent unchecked expansion by creating reactive world pressure
- Model the escalation of law enforcement and institutional response
- Create meaningful “lay low vs expand” strategic cycles
- Differentiate between organizational pressure and individual risk
- Avoid overloading assets with redundant “danger” or “risk” meters

It solves the problem of criminal empire growth becoming optimal in all cases by ensuring success generates consequences.

## Mechanic Description

Heat is divided into three interconnected layers:

### Rule 1: Faction Heat (Global Pressure)

Faction Heat represents the overall institutional pressure on the organization as a whole.

- It reflects how aggressively the city or world is targeting the faction.
- It is influenced by major crimes, public scandals, and sustained activity across territories.
- It affects all operations regardless of location.

Effects include:
- Increased investigation frequency
- Higher baseline patrol intensity across all territories
- Faster escalation from local incidents to faction-wide responses
- Greater chance of coordinated crackdowns

Faction Heat is a systemic value, not tied to any specific location.

---

### Rule 2: Territory Heat (Local Pressure)

Territory Heat represents institutional attention focused on a specific district or region.

- It reflects how dangerous it is to operate in that area.
- It is generated by local criminal activity and sustained operations.
- It can vary significantly between territories.

Effects include:
- Increased detection risk for actions in that territory
- Higher chance of witnesses and interruptions
- Reduced efficiency of illicit operations
- Increased Exposure gain for characters operating there

Territory Heat is spatial and encourages geographic strategy and redistribution of operations.

---

### Rule 3: Exposure (Character Risk State)

Exposure represents how identifiable and traceable a specific character is.

- It reflects how easily a character can be linked to criminal activity.
- It is influenced by actions, surveillance, and operating under pressure.
- It does not automatically spread between characters.

Effects include:
- Higher chance of arrest, assassination, or targeting
- Reduced ability to operate openly in high-Heat areas
- Increased interrogation value if captured
- Increased risk when performing visible actions

Exposure is personal and enables character-level risk management.

---

### Rule 4: Heat Interaction Rules

The three layers interact in controlled ways:

- High Faction Heat increases Exposure gain rates indirectly
- High Territory Heat increases Exposure gain for actions in that area
- Exposure only increases Faction Heat when it leads to high-profile events (e.g., capture, interrogation leaks)
- Territory Heat contributes slowly to Faction Heat if sustained over time
- Exposure does not propagate between characters

## Player Interaction & Tradeoffs

Players constantly manage risk across three layers:

- Decide where to operate (Territory Heat management)
- Decide who to deploy (Exposure management)
- Decide when to escalate or pause expansion (Faction Heat management)

Tradeoffs include:

- Expansion vs Safety
- High-value operations vs long-term Exposure risk
- Concentrating activity (efficiency) vs spreading risk geographically
- Using experienced agents (high Exposure risk) vs fresh operatives

## Progression (Optional)

### Early Game
- Low Heat across all layers
- Exposure is rare and mostly tied to failures
- Territory management is more forgiving

### Mid Game
- Heat becomes uneven across territories
- Exposure becomes a meaningful constraint on key characters
- Faction Heat begins shaping overall pacing

### Late Game
- High Faction Heat creates systemic pressure
- Territory Heat zones become strategically critical
- Exposure management becomes essential for elite operatives
- The faction must balance survival vs dominance

## Examples

### Example: Docks Heist

A crew performs a high-profile smuggling operation in the Docks.

- Territory Heat (Docks) increases significantly
- Participating agents gain Exposure due to witness presence
- If the operation is loud enough, Faction Heat increases slightly

Result:
- The Docks become temporarily dangerous
- One lieutenant must be rotated out due to Exposure
- The faction as a whole becomes slightly more pressured

---

### Example: Captured Lieutenant

A highly Exposed lieutenant is captured in a hot territory.

- Exposure triggers interrogation
- Interrogation reveals faction involvement
- Faction Heat increases significantly
- Territory Heat spikes in multiple connected districts

Result:
- Systemic crackdown begins
- Multiple operations are disrupted
- The faction enters a “lay low” strategic phase

## Design Notes

- Heat is intentionally split to avoid a single overloaded “wanted level” system
- Territory Heat creates spatial strategy and prevents abstraction
- Exposure creates character identity and rotation gameplay
- Faction Heat creates pacing and long-term tension
- The system is designed to generate cascading consequences without full randomness
- Avoid adding Heat layers to assets to prevent duplication and UI bloat
