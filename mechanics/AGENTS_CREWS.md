# Agents / Crews

## Core Concept
Crews are persistent operational units representing "goon groups" used to execute actions across the world.

They are the main execution layer of the game, bridging strategy and action.

---

## Structure
Organization → Lieutenants → Crews

- Crews are the atomic operational unit
- Crews are owned by Lieutenants
- Crews can be used by the organization at a cost

---

## Crew Properties
Each crew has:
- Type (role specialization)
- Size (abstract strength)
- Experience level
- Traits (limited modifiers with tradeoffs)
- Condition state (operational status)
- Exposure / Heat (visibility and risk)
- Loyalty drift (stability under strain)

---

## Acquisition & Growth
- Generated via districts, assets, and organizational control
- Evolve through mission participation
- Change state through use (experience, condition, exposure)

---

## Assignment Model
Crews can be assigned to:
- Missions (short-term actions)
- Operations (long-term effects)
- Territory roles (persistent control)

Assignment outside their controlling lieutenant may incur costs.

---

## Traits System
- Define specialization and behavior modifiers
- Always include tradeoffs, not pure bonuses
- Shape efficiency, detection, risk, and reward profiles

---

## Condition System
Crews degrade through use:
- Operational states represent readiness
- Damage and strain affect performance
- Recovery required to restore effectiveness

---

## Exposure System
- Actions generate visibility and heat
- Exposure increases risk and enemy awareness
- High-value crews become fragile over time

---

## Strategic Layers Enabled
- Risk management across operations
- Crew rotation and burnout dynamics
- Territorial specialization
- Emergent crew reputation
- Internal power dynamics via lieutenant control
