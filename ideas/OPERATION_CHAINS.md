# Exploration: Temporary Operations System (Discarded / Needs Careful Review)

**Status:** DISCARDED (may be revisited with strict constraints)

## Concept Summary

This idea explored a temporary “Operations” layer that groups multiple assets (POIs) into coordinated strategic structures using predefined templates. The goal was to create moments of multi-asset coordination, risk propagation, and high-stakes economic bursts without introducing a permanent network or production-chain system.

Unlike full logistics systems, Operations would not introduce new economic flows. Instead, they would act as a **temporary organizational overlay** that modifies how existing assets behave together under a shared strategic intent.

---

## Core Structure

### 1. Operation Templates (Core Idea)

Operations would be defined through fixed templates rather than free-form graphs.

Examples of templates:
- Smuggling Run
- Money Laundering Cycle
- Distribution Push
- Intelligence Sweep
- Protection Surge

Each template defines:
- Required asset roles (e.g. input / processing / output / support)
- Expected risk profile (low visibility vs high throughput)
- Synergy type (economic boost, Heat redistribution, stability tradeoffs)
- Duration constraints (temporary activation window)

Templates are intentionally **limited and predefined** to avoid optimization spirals and UI complexity.

---

## 2. Asset Assignment

Players assign existing assets (POIs) into operation roles:

- Dock → input role
- Warehouse → processing role
- Club → distribution role
- Safehouse → support role

Assets do not change their base mechanics.  
They only gain **contextual behavior modifiers while part of an operation**.

---

## 3. Tradeoffs

Operations introduce explicit strategic tradeoffs:

### Efficiency vs Stability
- Higher coordination yields better economic output
- But increases systemic fragility if disrupted

### Concentration vs Exposure
- Grouped assets become more efficient together
- But become easier to detect or destabilize as a cluster

### Local Safety vs Cross-District Risk
- Operations spanning multiple territories are stronger
- But significantly more vulnerable to interruption and Heat escalation

---

## 4. Operation Cost / Capacity Pressure (Key Limiting Factor)

This system relies heavily on **scarce organizational capacity** as a balancing mechanism.

Operations are not free to maintain:

### Costs may include:
- Limited number of active operations per faction
- Leadership bandwidth (Lieutenants / HQ capacity)
- Agent allocation required to stabilize or maintain operations
- Increased Heat “maintenance cost” while active

### Resulting tension:
Players must choose between:
- Running coordinated high-efficiency operations
- OR investing capacity into:
  - territorial control
  - defense / security
  - expansion
  - agent actions

This ensures Operations are not a default optimization tool, but a **strategic allocation of limited attention**.

---

## 5. Risk Propagation (Light Version)

While active, operations create linked vulnerability:

- Disruption of one asset reduces efficiency of the whole operation
- Heat or attacks partially propagate across involved assets
- However, assets remain functional independently if the operation fails

This avoids full system collapse while still creating meaningful risk coupling.

---

## Design Evaluation

### What worked conceptually:
- Strong idea of multi-asset coordination without explicit logistics simulation
- Potential for emergent “criminal network identity”
- Interesting high-risk / high-reward framing
- Good synergy with Heat and Agent systems (in theory)

### Core problems identified:
- Tends toward shallow buff system if not heavily constrained
- Risks overlapping with existing systems (Heat, Agents, Territory control)
- Creates cognitive load and tracking issues if persistent
- Becomes hard to maintain clarity when multiple operations exist

---

## Final Verdict

This system was **discarded in its current form** due to:
- redundancy with existing mechanics
- lack of unique decision axis
- risk of cognitive overload and systemic noise

However, the underlying idea remains valuable in a limited form if reframed around:
- strict capacity constraints
- temporary activation windows
- high-cost organizational commitment
- non-persistent structural coupling

Further exploration should only proceed if it introduces a **new decision axis not already covered by Heat, Agents, or Territory systems**.
